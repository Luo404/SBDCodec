SBDCodec: Decoupling Speech and Background-sound in Codec To Allow Explicit Feature Selections for Downstream Audio Tasks.

Xiaoxue Luo1,2, Jinwei Huang1,2, Zihao Cui1,2, Tao Li1,2, Yanan Chen1,2, Yingying Gao1,2, Shilei Zhang1,2,*, Junlan Feng1,2.

1China Mobile Research Institute, China

2State Key Laboratory of Multimedia Information Processing, Peking University, China

Abstract

Recently, unifying framework for processing various audio tasks has become a trend, while different audio tasks concern about the features of distinct audio components. Therefore, this paper propose a SBDCodec that decouples speech and background-sound tokens during input audio discretization, so that downstream audio tasks are enable to select efficient input tokens explicitly. To achieve completely decoupling, at the model level, we design parallel independent speech and background-sound vector quantization modules, and at the training level, the propose token swapping training method with several other decoupling techniques are utilized.
